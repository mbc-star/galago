01. The Problem

Your team is spending
mission time on administration

Every scholarship foundation faces the same structural challenge. The work that matters — students, schools, sponsors — competes daily with the work that doesn't: filing, reporting, searching, rebuilding.

Sponsor reporting takes hours. Every update to a donor means hunting across folders, copying figures, reformatting photos. For 40+ sponsors, that is weeks of team time per year.
No early warning on students. A student missing school or dropping grades goes unnoticed until the term review. By then, the window to intervene has often closed.
The programme is hard to explain. When a foundation board, a funder, or a new partner asks "how are your students doing?", the answer requires assembling data from multiple places.
Knowledge lives in people, not systems. When a key team member leaves, they take institutional memory with them. Student history, sponsor context, school relationships: all at risk.
